AI PM Skills Checklist

24 skills that separate AI-ready PMs from the rest

How to use: Check off skills you're confident in. Be honest — this is for you. Count your checks at the end to see where you stand.
1 AI Foundations
Probabilistic vs Deterministic Logic
Understand why AI outputs vary and how to manage uncertainty in product specs
LLM Capabilities & Limitations
Know what LLMs can/can't do — avoid overpromising to stakeholders
AI Impact Assessment
Map existing product features to AI feasibility and business impact
Model Selection Basics
Know when to use GPT-4 vs Claude vs open-source models
2 Hands-on Building
CustomGPT / Agent Creation
Build functional AI agents with instructions, knowledge files, and tools
Advanced Prompting (CoT, Few-Shot)
Write prompts that produce consistent, high-quality outputs
Multimodal AI (Vision + Audio)
Use vision models for UI analysis, audio for transcription/voice
Rapid UI Prototyping
Build functional prototypes using v0.dev, Lovable, or similar tools
API & Webhook Integration
Connect LLMs to real-world triggers using Zapier/Make
Agentic Logic Design
Design flows for autonomous, tool-using AI agents
3 AI Product Strategy
AI-Powered User Research
Use AI for sentiment analysis and synthesizing large feedback datasets
AI-Driven Prioritization
Apply AI to RICE/Kano scoring with strategic weighting
Writing AI Feature Specs
Define acceptance criteria for probabilistic features
AI Roadmap Planning
Build a realistic AI product roadmap stakeholders can trust
Cost-Quality Tradeoffs
Balance API costs, latency, and output quality in production
4 Safety & Quality
Hallucination Management
Identify, prevent, and mitigate AI hallucinations in production
Bias Detection & Mitigation
Recognize bias patterns and implement guardrails
Red Teaming AI Features
Test AI products for edge cases and failure modes before launch
AI Compliance Basics
Understand data privacy, copyright, and regulatory considerations
5 AI Leadership
Stakeholder AI Communication
Explain AI feasibility, timelines, and limitations to non-technical leaders
AI Team Collaboration
Work effectively with ML engineers, data scientists, and AI researchers
AI Vendor Evaluation
Assess build vs buy decisions for AI capabilities
AI Strategy Pitching
Present AI initiatives with clear ROI and strategic rationale
Staying Current
Have a system for tracking AI developments relevant to your product
