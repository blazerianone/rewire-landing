WHAT HIRING MANAGERS ACTUALLY LOOK FOR

90 sec

I've sat on the hiring side. I've interviewed hundreds of candidates. Let me tell you what we're actually evaluating.

It's not whether you can code. It's not whether you have a CS degree.

It's three things:

1
Probabilistic thinking.
This is the #1 reason candidates fail AI PM interviews. Traditional products are deterministic — if user clicks A, system does B. AI products are probabilistic — the model will be right 95% of the time. How do you design for the 5%? What's the acceptable error rate? How do you fail gracefully? If you still think like a traditional PM, you'll try to "fix" the 5% like a bug. AI PMs design for uncertainty.
2
Proof.
Can you show your work? When I ask "tell me about an AI product decision you made" — do you have something to point to? A document you wrote? A product you shipped? Something that shows how you think? Everyone has opinions about AI. Proof is what separates you.
3
Collaboration.
Can you work with ML engineers without being intimidated? Can you push back when they over-engineer something? Can you ask the right questions? You don't need to do their job. You need to partner with them.

None of this requires a CS degree. None of this requires coding. What it requires is learning how to think about AI products. And that's a skill you can build.

Energy: Authoritative but reassuring. Insider knowledge.
